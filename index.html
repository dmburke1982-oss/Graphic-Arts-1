Welcome & What Is Graphic Design?

Introduction to Visual Communication

Today's Brief:

Today you'll learn what graphic design is and why it matters. You'll look at everyday designs, discuss what they communicate, and start thinking like a designer.

Learning Objectives

  • • Define graphic design in practical terms
  • • Identify design in everyday life
  • • Practice respectful discussion and critique

Bell Ringer

5 min

Write down three designs you saw this morning. What were they trying to do?

Mini Lesson

15 min
  • • What graphic design is (and isn't)
  • • Design as communication and problem-solving
  • • Examples: posters, apps, packaging, signage

Activity: Design Spotting

20 min

Students list or sketch examples from the room or memory. Small-group discussion: purpose, audience, effectiveness.

Exit Ticket

In one sentence, explain what graphic design does.

Visual Language & Observation

Training Your Designer's Eye

Today's Brief:

Designers notice details. Today you'll train your eye to observe shapes, spacing, and relationships.

Bell Ringer

5 min

Look at one object near you. List five visual details without naming the object.

Activity: Blind Contour + Object Study

25 min

Blind contour drawing (no looking at paper). Second drawing with careful observation. Brief comparison and discussion.

Exit Ticket

One thing you noticed today that you usually ignore.

Intro Design Challenge & Reflection

Applying What You've Learned

Today's Brief:

You'll apply what you learned this week in a small design challenge and reflect on your thinking.

Activity: Personal Symbol Design

35 min

Prompt: Create a simple symbol that represents you using only shapes. Sketch ideas. Choose one and refine. Focus on clarity and intention.

  • • What worked?
  • • What was challenging?
  • • What would you improve?

Exit Ticket
